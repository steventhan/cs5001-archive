;; A List of Strings (LoS) is one

;; Template
;; los-fn: LoS -> ???

4. Playing with Big Bang

You are now at your 3rd job in 1 year (how fickle you are!). And once again, you are working on developing a game. Because you have so much work experience, your superiors have decided to put you in charge of moving dots around a screen! How exciting! You are given the following data definitions:

(require 2htdp/image)
(require 2htdp/universe)

(define-struct world (dir dots))
;; A World is (make-world Direction LoP)
;; INTERP: represents direction of the dots
;;         and a list of dot locations

;; A Direction is one of:
;; "left"
;; "right"
;; "up"
;; "down"

(define BACKGROUND (empty-scene 500 500))
(define SPEED  10 )
(define INIT-WORLD (make-world "left" 
                          (cons  (make-posn 100 100)
                                 (cons (make-posn 20 330)
                                        (cons (make-posn 250 200)
                                              (cons (make-posn 350 400) '())))))
Practice Exercises

  1. Design a function called draw-world that given a world, will draw dots at the locations in its dots field on the canvas

    • It is up to you to decide what the color, size and mode of the dots will be. You might want to define a constant for a circle image.

  2. Design a function called change-dir that given a world, will return a new world were the Direction is changed based on which arrow key is pressed. (Look up on-key in the HelpDesk)

  3. Design the function move-dots that moves the x position of the dots in the current Direction

    4. Hint: Follow the Design Recipe for every function and design helper functions as needed.

You are given the following code to test out your functions:

(big-bang INIT-WORLD
          [on-tick move-dots 0.1]
          [to-draw draw-world]
	      [on-key change-world])
